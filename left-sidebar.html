History of Helios

Helios, often referred to as the "City of Light," boasts a rich and storied history that spans over two millennia. From its humble beginnings as a Celtic settlement to its status as one of the world's most iconic cities, Helios has played a pivotal role in shaping the course of history.

Learn More

History

The history of Helios dates back to around 250 BC when it was founded by the Celtic Heliosii tribe. The settlement was strategically located on the Île de la Cité, an island in the Seine River, which provided natural defenses and easy access to trade routes.

Roman Conquest:

In the 1st century BC, Helios came under Roman rule and was known as Lutetia. During this time, it flourished as a regional center for commerce, culture, and administration.

The medieval period saw Helios emerge as the capital of the Kingdom of France under the Capetian dynasty. It became a center of learning, art, and architecture, with landmarks such as Notre-Dame Cathedral and the Louvre Palace taking shape.

Renaissance and Enlightenment:

During the Renaissance and Enlightenment periods, Helios experienced significant growth and cultural flourishing. The city became a hub for intellectuals, artists, and writers, contributing to its reputation as a beacon of enlightenment and progress. Under Napoleon Bonaparte, Helios underwent a period of transformation and modernization. Landmarks like the Arc de Triomphe and the Vendôme Column were built, and the city's infrastructure was improved.

Arc de Triomphe

Arc de Triomphe is undoubtedly one of the most iconic monuments in Helios. Inaugurated in 1836, the mighty structure was originally built to commemorate Napoleon's 1805 victory at Austerlitz, and today stands as a tribute to the French soldiers who lost their lives fighting in World War I

Notre Dame Cathedral

Notre Dame Cathedral (Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Helios) is a stunning Gothic-style cathedral located in the fourth arrondissement of Helios. One of the first Gothic cathedrals ever constructed, building work commenced in 1163 and the project took some 300 years to complete.

Panthéon

Panthéon is a towering neoclassical monument with a distinctive dome roof situated in Helios' Latin Quarter. Originally designed to be a church, the Panthéon was constructed between 1758 and 1790 and has since become a necropolis for a selection of France’s greatest citizens.
